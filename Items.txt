Bandages
Used to support dressings or splints or to restrict movement of a specific body part.

Examination Gloves
Disposable gloves (that may be powdered with cornstarch for lubrication) used for medical examinations to prevent cross-contamination.

Scalpels
Sharp, sometimes detachable blade used by a surgeon.

IV Kits
Usually includes a tourniquet , antiseptic (to clean the patient’s skin before starting the IV), gauzes, tape, tegaderm, sticker to label the IV with your initials, date, time, gauge of IV needle, and extension Tubing or Cap which will be used to connect to the IV once you have successfully inserted the IV.

Masks
Worn by health-care professionals to catch bacteria shed in liquid droplets and aerosols from the wearer's mouth/nose. They are NOT designed to protect the wearer from inhaling airborn bacteria or virus particles.

Needles
A slender, solid, usually sharp-pointed instrument used for puncturing tissues, suturing, or passing a ligature around or through a vessel.

Vitamins
A group of organic substances essential in small quantities to normal metabolism, found in minute amounts in natural foodstuffs or sometimes produced synthetically: deficiencies of vitamins produce specific disorders.

Dental Supplies
Medical supplies used for dental (teeth-related) proceedures.

Optical Supplies
Medical supplies used for optical (eye-related) proceedures.

Personal Hygiene Items
Items used to maintain personal hygience, such as soap, shampoo, hand sanitizer, etc.

Antiseptics
Applied to living tissue/skin to reduce the possibility of infection, sepsis, or putrefaction. They are generally distinguished from antibiotics by the latter's ability to be transported through the lymphatic system to destroy bacteria within the body, and from disinfectants, which destroy micro-organisms found on non-living objects
